History of Education

The American education system evolved from early common schools and locally funded efforts to a complex network of public, private, and charter institutions. Landmark policies—from compulsory schooling to desegregation and federal accountability—have shaped access, quality, and expectations.

Today, the legacy of these shifts lives on in debates about standards, assessments, teacher preparation, and the role of families and communities in student success.
